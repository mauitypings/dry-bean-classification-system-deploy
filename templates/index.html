01 . Academic System Profile

About ProBean AI

ProBean AI is an interactive, web-based dry bean classification terminal developed as a final project milestone for IT20/L (Professional Track for IT 6) under the supervision of Iris Mae C. Mendoza, MIT. Traditional physical inspections across large agricultural outputs can introduce systemic observation blockages, manual variation, and substantial labor costs.

By taking advantage of the verified Dry Bean Dataset (comprising 13,611 unique samples) originally published by Köklü and Özkan (2020), this system allows users to bypass expensive computer vision clusters or scanning hardware. It offers a fast, accessible platform for automated prediction based on standard morphological parameters.

03 . Workflow Architecture

How Does ProBean AI Work?

Follow these three simple steps to run an instant variety analysis.

1
📐

Input Morphological Metrics

Log in to your terminal account or proceed as a guest. Open the prediction form and enter the geometric dimensions of your sample (such as Area, Perimeter, and Axis Lengths).

2
🌳

Ensemble Tree Processing

The backend forwards your input values to our serialized Random Forest pipeline. The model tests your values against thousands of calibrated data points simultaneously.

3
🎯

The system displays your estimated strain variety along with a calculated prediction confidence score. Registered accounts can save these records directly to their history table.

04 . Classification Index

Supported Bean Varieties

The underlying Random Forest model performs accurate multiclass sorting across 7 distinct dry bean types based on structural profiles.

01 . Cultivar Variety

Seker

Highly spherical and symmetric dimensions. Noticeable for clean uniform roundness scores across size metrics, separating easily from more elongated grain contours.

02 . Cultivar Variety

Barbunya

Characterized by prominent oval-to-round geometric profiles, holding distinct ratio values. Its balanced cross-sectional shape factors make it a standard marker within our multivariable tracking configuration matrices.

03 . Cultivar Variety

Bombay

The largest physical strain profile. Massive area and perimeter distributions compared to standard samples. Due to its outlier nature regarding surface scaling dimensions, it features near-perfect classification sensitivity scores.

04 . Cultivar Variety

Dermason

Medium-to-small structured shapes. Represents one of the most structurally dense, balanced profiles within our verification system, maintaining high uniformity.

05 . Cultivar Variety

Cali

Distinct elongated curved shapes showing an elevated major axis length paired with specialized eccentricities. These extended architectural parameters make them distinctly easier for localized decision nodes to sort out instantly.

06 . Cultivar Variety

Horoz

Highly linear and oblong grain structure. Exhibits a significantly long major axis coupled with narrow minor dimension widths, providing clear identification signatures.

07 . Cultivar Variety

Sira

Smaller elliptical profile. Possesses highly stable, consistent solidity and roundness parameters that map predictably across ensemble evaluation branches.

06 . Common Inquiries

Frequently Asked Questions

What structural parameters does the Random Forest model require?

The system processes standard morphological variables from geometric images. These include core parameters like Area, Perimeter, Major Axis Length, and Minor Axis Length, along with shape factors like Solidity, Roundness, and Compactness.

Can I test out features without creating an account?

Yes. Guest mode lets you run immediate classifications through our Random Forest model. However, you will need to sign in to unlock your persistent history table and access dashboard chart insights.

Why did you choose Random Forest over other options?

During project development, we experimented with several classification methods. The Random Forest algorithm delivered the most stable performance across all 7 bean varieties, achieving a testing accuracy of 91.99% without overfitting.
